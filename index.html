Benefits and source

Black seeds are also known as black caraway, black cumin, kalonji, and black onion seeds. They come from Nigella sativa, a small plant with pale purple, blue, or white flowers that grows in Eastern Europe, Western Asia, and the Middle East. Black seed oil has shown promise in treating some of the most common health conditions, including high blood pressure and asthma.

Usage

It's usually lightly toasted and then ground or used whole to add flavor to bread or curry dishes. Some people also eat the seeds raw or mix them with honey or water. It can also be used for hair and skin care.
